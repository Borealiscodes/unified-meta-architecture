# Structural Plane Overview
*The plane that defines what exists, independent of interpretation.*

---

**Purpose:** Establish the foundational structures, entities, and components that UMA recognizes before governance, ethics, or documentation are applied.

---


# Structural Plane Overview
The Structural Plane describes the “shape” of a system — what exists, how it is arranged, and how components relate.

## 1. Purpose
The Structural Plane provides a clear, non‑interpretive map of:
- components  
- boundaries  
- relationships  
- flows  
- positions  

It answers the question: **“What is here?”**

## 2. What Belongs in the Structural Plane
- diagrams  
- maps  
- layouts  
- component lists  
- relationship graphs  
- boundaries and interfaces  

No governance, ethics, or interpretation belongs here.

## 3. What Does NOT Belong Here
- rules  
- permissions  
- constraints  
- ethical framing  
- psychological meaning  
- metaphysical claims  

Those belong to other planes.

## 4. Structural Integrity Rules
To maintain clarity:
- keep diagrams descriptive, not prescriptive  
- avoid mixing governance or ethics into structural notes  
- represent only what exists, not what should exist  
- maintain consistent naming across components  

## 5. Allowed Operations
- mapping  
- diagramming  
- listing components  
- defining boundaries  
- describing flows  

## 6. Prohibited Operations
- assigning authority  
- defining rules  
- implying hierarchy  
- embedding ethical meaning  

## 7. One‑Sentence Summary
The Structural Plane is the clean, descriptive map of what exists in a system — nothing more.
