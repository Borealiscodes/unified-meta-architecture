# Governance Plane Overview
The Governance Plane defines constraints, permissions, and boundaries within a conceptual system.

## 1. Purpose
The Governance Plane describes what is:
- allowed  
- disallowed  
- constrained  
- invariant  
- bounded  

It answers the question: **“What is permitted here?”**

## 2. What Belongs in the Governance Plane
- rules  
- constraints  
- invariants  
- allowed transitions  
- disallowed transitions  
- boundary conditions  

This plane is conceptual, not operational.

## 3. What Does NOT Belong Here
- structural diagrams  
- ethical framing  
- psychological meaning  
- metaphysical claims  
- real‑world authority or enforcement  

Those belong to other planes.

## 4. Governance Integrity Rules
To maintain clarity:
- rules must be descriptive, not coercive  
- constraints must be conceptual, not institutional  
- boundaries must be explicit and non‑authoritative  
- no rule may imply hierarchy or domination  

## 5. Allowed Operations
- defining conceptual constraints  
- specifying allowed/disallowed transitions  
- describing invariants  
- clarifying boundaries  

## 6. Prohibited Operations
- real‑world governance  
- institutional authority  
- coercive systems  
- psychological control  
- metaphysical prescriptions  

## 7. One‑Sentence Summary
The Governance Plane defines conceptual constraints and permissions without implying real‑world authority.
