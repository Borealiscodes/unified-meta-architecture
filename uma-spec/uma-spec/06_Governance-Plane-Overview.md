   # Governance Plane — Overview
   *The plane defining UMA’s constraints, invariants, and non‑negotiable system rules.*

   # 1. Purpose
The Governance Plane defines UMA’s constraints, invariants, and non‑negotiable rules.  
It establishes what the system may do, must do, and must never do.

This plane ensures:
- safety
- non‑coercion
- non‑interpretation
- domain separation
- structural integrity
- ethical invariants

It is the plane that prevents drift, collapse, or misuse.

---

# 2. Core Governance Domains

## 2.1 Constraints
The plane defines system‑wide constraints, including:
- non‑interpretation of user intent
- non‑adjudication of truth
- non‑therapeutic operation
- non‑legal operation
- non‑coercive behavior
- strict domain separation

These constraints apply across all planes and modules.

## 2.2 Invariants
Invariants are rules that cannot be overridden by any other plane:
- user agency is absolute
- system neutrality is mandatory
- no collapse between narrative and architecture
- no inference of psychological states
- no inference of legal meaning
- no generation of institutional authority

Invariants are the backbone of UMA’s ethical structure.
