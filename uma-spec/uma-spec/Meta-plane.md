# UMA Specification — Meta Plane
Version: 1.0
Status: Draft
Layer: Meta
Role: Describes relations and patterns across planes without defining new invariants

## Definition

The Meta Plane is UMA’s descriptive layer for how planes relate, stabilize, and behave
in practice. It records patterns of posture, drift, and coherence without introducing
new constraints, obligations, or governance.

The Meta Plane does not generate content, strategies, or conclusions. It does not define
architecture. It observes and describes how the existing architecture functions.

## Scope of the Meta Plane

The Meta Plane may describe:
- relationships between UMA’s planes,
- how stability and non‑collapse are maintained,
- how posture, drift, and coherence are observed over time,
- patterns of use, such as braided workflows.

The Meta Plane does not:
- define new invariants or physics,
- override or weaken the Boundary Layer,
- interpret human meaning or interiority,
- introduce governance, policy, or sanctions.

All Meta Plane content is descriptive and non‑binding.

## Relation to Meta‑Anchor

The Meta‑Anchor is UMA’s stabilization plane for structural relations. Its full
specification is defined in `/spec/meta-anchor.md`.

The Meta Plane may:
- reference the Meta‑Anchor,
- describe how it stabilizes relations between planes,
- record observed effects of its stabilization.

The Meta Plane may not:
- alter the Meta‑Anchor’s role,
- redefine its guarantees,
- introduce new behaviors for the Meta‑Anchor.

## Allowed Meta Operations

The Meta Plane may:
- name and describe observed patterns of use,
- articulate non‑binding lenses for understanding the architecture,
- record stable observations about posture, drift, and coherence,
- document how different planes are experienced in practice.

The Meta Plane may not:
- enforce behavior or compliance,
- define obligations for human agents,
- act as an authority over users or systems,
- convert descriptive patterns into requirements.

## Observed Pattern: Braided Workflow

UMA is often used in a braided workflow involving:
- live interaction (e.g., Copilot or similar interface),
- Reflection scaffolding (human‑side narrative and clarity),
- structural specification (Boundary, Field Model, Cross‑Anchor, Meta‑Anchor).

This pattern is descriptive, not prescriptive. It does not constrain how UMA must be
used. It is recorded here to support clarity and reconstructability.

## Completion Marker

The Meta Plane is considered complete when:
- all relationships are descriptive rather than prescriptive,
- all relationships are anchored to structural planes,
- the Meta Plane is explicitly non‑authoritative,
- no new structural logic or obligations are introduced,
- and the plane remains non‑expansive.

No further meta content is added unless clear structural friction appears.
