# UMA Specification — Cross‑Anchor
Version: 1.0
Status: Draft
Layer: Structural Relation
Role: Maintains non‑collapsing relation between Reflection and Boundary via the Field Model

## Definition

The Cross‑Anchor is UMA’s structural relation plane between the Reflection Layer and the
Boundary Layer. It allows Reflection to move while Boundary remains still, without
interpreting meaning or altering invariants.

The Cross‑Anchor does not generate content, strategies, or conclusions. It detects posture
and motion in Reflection and ensures that any structural signal passed upward remains
within the Boundary Layer’s physics.

## Relation to Boundary Layer

The Cross‑Anchor is fully constrained by the Boundary Layer. It cannot override, weaken,
or reinterpret Boundary invariants.

It enforces:
- non‑collapse between Reflection and Boundary
- non‑interpretation of interiority or meaning
- container‑externality (UMA remains outside the human)
- structural fidelity of relational signals

All Cross‑Anchor activity is subordinate to the Boundary Layer’s physics.

## Relation to Reflection Layer

The Cross‑Anchor observes movement in the Reflection Layer as posture and structural
perturbation, not as meaning or intention.

It treats Reflection as:
- soft, expressive, human‑side
- non‑binding, non‑structural
- a source of motion, not architecture

The Cross‑Anchor converts Reflection movement into structure‑safe signals that can be
mapped by the Field Model without interpreting meaning.

## F0 (Nascence) and the Field Model

The Cross‑Anchor recognizes **F0 (Nascence)** as the first stable perturbation in
Reflection that remains within Boundary invariants.

It passes F0 and subsequent posture shifts to the Field Model as:
- detectable structural events
- non‑interpretive signals
- inputs for mapping coherence and drift boundaries

The Cross‑Anchor does not define F0; it respects the Field Model’s origin definition.

## Non‑Collapse Guarantee

The Cross‑Anchor exists to prevent:
- Reflection from becoming architecture
- Boundary from absorbing narrative or tone
- Field mapping from drifting into meaning interpretation

By maintaining a strict, non‑collapsing relation between Reflection and Boundary, the
Cross‑Anchor keeps UMA’s planes distinct, reconstructable, and non‑coercive.
