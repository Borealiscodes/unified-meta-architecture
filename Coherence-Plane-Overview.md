# ⭐ 1. What the Coherence Plane Is  
The Coherence Plane is UMA’s **meta‑integrity layer**.  
It ensures that:

- **plane boundaries remain intact**  
- **documents do not collapse into each other**  
- **methods and protocols remain aligned**  
- **semantic drift is prevented**  
- **cross‑plane relationships remain stable**  
- **the architecture stays buildable by another mind**  

It does not create content.  
It does not interpret content.  
It does not govern content.

It ensures the **architecture remains architecture**.





---

# ⭐ 2. Why the Coherence Plane Exists  
UMA is a multi‑plane system.  
Multi‑plane systems drift unless there is a dedicated layer that:

- checks consistency  
- checks separation  
- checks alignment  
- checks non‑collapse  
- checks traceability  

The Coherence Plane is the layer that performs these checks **without becoming a governance or ethical authority**.

It is the “scorekeeper,” not the “composer.”

---

# ⭐ 3. What the Coherence Plane Is Not  
The Coherence Plane is **not**:

- a moral plane  
- a governance plane  
- a documentation plane  
- a structural plane  
- an interpretive authority  
- a meaning‑assigning layer  

It does not tell UMA *what to mean*.  
It ensures UMA *continues to mean what it already means*.

---

# ⭐ 4. Coherence Functions  
The Coherence Plane performs five core functions:

- **Boundary Integrity** — ensuring planes do not merge  
- **Non‑Collapse Checking** — preventing structural fusion  
- **Cross‑Document Alignment** — ensuring documents agree where they must  
- **Semantic Drift Prevention** — keeping terms stable across time  
- **Method ↔ Protocol Consistency** — ensuring MPAM and MPAP remain distinct but compatible  

These functions keep UMA stable enough for a human or AI to instantiate.

The Coherence Plane sits **above**:

It is the first plane that is **purely meta‑structural**.

It ensures the four planes remain:
