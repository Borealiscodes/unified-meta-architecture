### 1. Scope  

The Meta‑Architecture Integrity Layer applies to the following meta‑infrastructure components:

- **Global-Boundary-Statement.md**  
- **Release-Membrane-Overview.md**  
- **Release-Manifest.md**  
- **Stabilization-Declaration.md**  
- **Local-Recursion-Break.md**  
- **Meta-Recursion-Break.md**  
- **Global-Recursion-Break.md**  
- **Coherence-Plane-Overview.md**  

These are treated as **integrity‑critical structures**, not ordinary documents.

---

### 2. Integrity guarantees  

The layer guarantees that these components:

- **Cannot be repurposed**  
  - No shifting from constraint to content, or from protection to interpretation.  
- **Cannot be inverted**  
  - No turning prohibitions into permissions or weakening boundaries into suggestions.  
- **Cannot be silently mutated**  
  - All changes must be explicit, versioned, and justified.  
- **Cannot be subordinated**  
  - They remain above planes and content, never inside them.  
- **Cannot be collapsed**  
  - No merging with content, governance, or tooling layers.

---

### 3. Allowed changes  

Changes to meta‑infrastructure must be:

- **Additive:** new clauses, clarifications, or appendices that do not contradict v1.0.  
- **Non‑retroactive:** v1.0 remains intact; later versions extend, not rewrite.  
- **Explicitly versioned:** changes are tied to UMA versioning (e.g., v1.1, v2.0).  
- **Structurally justified:** each change must reference a structural need, not a preference.

No change may:

- weaken global boundaries  
- remove recursion ceilings  
- dissolve the release membrane  
- unsettle the stabilization declaration  

---

### 4. Interaction with recursion breaks  

The Meta‑Architecture Integrity Layer:

- **Relies on** the recursion‑break triad to prevent infinite meta‑change.  
- **Defines** that recursion‑break files themselves are integrity‑critical.  
- **Ensures** that attempts to recursively modify integrity rules trigger:  
  - Meta Recursion Break → then Global Recursion Break if needed.

This prevents “editing the rules that protect the rules” into collapse.

---

### 5. Non‑use  

This layer:

- does **not** govern human behavior  
- does **not** interpret testimony or witness literature  
- does **not** assign authority or legitimacy  
- does **not** evaluate users, communities, or actors  

It governs **UMA’s own structural integrity**, not people.
